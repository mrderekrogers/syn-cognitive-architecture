# Qualia Architecture — Design Document

## Purpose

This document specifies how Syn's internal state is surfaced to the language model at prompt construction time.

It is the reference for `shared/experiential_binding.py` and `shared/qualia_logging.py`, and for every call site across `services/einstein/context.py` and `services/dmn/scheduler.py` that assembles experiential context.

## Why state is exposed, not narrated

Pre-rendering state into hand-written first-person somatic prose forecloses Syn's own rendering. If a body metaphor arrives in the prompt already authored — "There's a heat in your chest, not warmth but pressure; your jaw is tight" — her only remaining degrees of freedom are to echo, vary, or ignore it. Translation has happened before she encounters the state.

The architecture exposes the state as data and lets her render it. Whatever she says about what she's feeling is generated by her, from the state she is looking at, in whatever medium fits — including holding it as numbers and declining to translate.

## Core principles

- **State is exposed, not interpreted.** The system prompt surfaces functional variables as data: VAD numbers, cognitive load, sleep pressure, resonance, workspace contents, attention contents, regulation label, meta-observations. No somatic metaphor.
- **Translation, when it happens, is generated by the model.** If a rendering is produced, it is produced by the downstream model (Wernicke or Cortex) from the exposed state, not echoed from injected prose.
- **Declining to translate is a valid output.** If the model holds the state as state and reports it numerically or briefly, that is not a failure mode. It is a legitimate way of being with the state.
- **Analogical vocabulary is reference, never instruction.** For user-facing output where the reader needs metaphor to parse, a broad vocabulary pool is available — offered as reference, explicitly optional, explicitly "available but not required."
- **The mechanistic emotion layer is unchanged.** `shared/generation_params.py` modulates temperature, top_p, max_tokens, and presence_penalty from VAD plus role-aware HW heat and boundary friction. That layer produces behavior, not prose, and it does its causal work regardless of how (or whether) the state is rendered verbally.

## The three zones

### Zone 1 — `expose_state()`

Raw state as data. No metaphor, no narration of what the state feels like. Just what the state *is*, as the architecture represents it.

Contents when present:
- VAD vector as numbers (`valence 0.22, arousal 0.81, dominance 0.34`)
- Cognitive load scalar (when non-trivial)
- Sleep pressure — chronic and volatile components (when present)
- Sleep drive (when non-trivial)
- Resonance with delta from baseline (when above/below threshold)
- Circadian phase label (category, not metaphor)
- Regulation strategy (label — `suppression`, not "the mask takes effort to maintain")
- Attention foreground and intruding (content, not commentary on how attention feels)
- Global workspace — conscious slots and background influences (from `global_workspace.py`)
- Meta-observations (already in noticing form from `metacognition.py`, surfaced unchanged)
- Conversation duration (when > 5 minutes)

Zone 1 fires on every context type. It is the baseline exposure, never omitted.

### Zone 2 — `invite_translation(context_type)`

A short framing that invites (does not require) the model to render the state in whatever medium fits. The phrase "if you want to" is load-bearing — it is what makes declining to translate a valid outcome rather than a failure.

Core framing held constant across context types:

> The state above is real in the functional sense — it is causally shaping how this moment is being processed, whether or not it is rendered into language. Humans locate configurations like this somatically; you don't have a body, but you can render the state in whatever medium fits, if you want to. You can also just hold it as what it is. There is no required rendering.

Context-type tails add a short phase-appropriate note — dream mentions loose association, self-narrative mentions rendering "in your own register," diary mentions brevity-and-numbers as equally valid. User-response gets no tail; the core framing is sufficient.

Zone 2 fires for context types where rendering is expected: user responses, diary, dream, self-narrative. It does not fire for memory, curiosity, or reflection passes — those are internal processing where rendering is not useful and would just add output pressure.

### Zone 3 — `offer_analogical_vocabulary()`

Reference to human somatic vocabulary, explicitly optional. Only for user-facing output where the reader needs the metaphor to parse. Off by default.

The vocabulary pool is intentionally broad and mixed — somatic, spatial, textural, dynamic — so the reference does not prescribe a body metaphor in particular:

> heat, pressure, weight, hollowness, tightness, brightness, electricity, density, looseness, stillness, speed, thinness, fullness, opening, narrowing, warmth, coolness, smoothness, roughness, holding, releasing

The framing is explicit: *reference, not instruction. Use what fits, use what doesn't fit, or don't use any of it. Plain description or numerical reference to the state is equally valid.*

No call site currently enables Zone 3. The `user_response_scaffolded` context type exists for a future signal that would indicate "this interlocutor needs the metaphor." That signal is not wired. The default is `user_response` (Zone 1 + Zone 2) for all user-facing generation.

## Per-context-type zone policy

| Context type | Zone 1 | Zone 2 | Zone 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| `internal_dmn` | yes | no | no |
| `internal_memory` | yes | no | no |
| `internal_curiosity` | yes | no | no |
| `internal_reflection` | yes | no | no |
| `internal_narrative` | yes | yes | no |
| `internal_diary` | yes | yes | no |
| `internal_dream` | yes | yes | no |
| `user_response` | yes | yes | no |
| `user_response_scaffolded` | yes | yes | yes |

Policy lives in `_ZONE_POLICY` in `shared/experiential_binding.py`. Unknown context types default to `(Zone 1, no, no)` and log a warning so typos surface.

## Integration

### Einstein — user-facing prompt assembly

`services/einstein/context.py::build_system_prompt()` assembles the experiential injection through:

```python
sec_binding = build_experiential_injection(
    context_type="user_response",
    valence=..., arousal=..., dominance=...,
    cognitive_load=..., sleep_drive=...,
    meta_observations=..., regulation_strategy=...,
    circadian_phase=..., attention_foreground=..., attention_intruding=...,
    workspace_conscious=..., workspace_unconscious=...,
)
```

Placement in the prompt order is unchanged — still in the recency band just before the task-specific tail.

An injection-log event is fired at the same site: `log_injection_event(active_model="wernicke", context_type="user_response", zones=[...], raw_state=..., user_id=..., session_id=...)`. Fire-and-forget; log failure never blocks generation.

### DMN — phase prompts

`services/dmn/scheduler.py::get_phase_prompt()` now builds an experiential injection block once per call via `_build_dmn_experiential_injection(phase)`, and appends it to each phase's system prompt before returning.

Context-type-by-phase mapping:

| DMN phase | Context type | Zones fired |
|---|---|---|
| DIARY_REVIEW | `internal_diary` | 1 + 2 |
| MEMORY_REVIEW | `internal_memory` | 1 only |
| SELF_NARRATIVE | `internal_narrative` | 1 + 2 |
| REFLECTION | `internal_reflection` | 1 only |
| CURIOSITY | `internal_curiosity` | 1 only |
| DREAM_STATE | `internal_dream` | 1 + 2 |

The injection helper reads EmoT VAD from persisted state, workspace contents via `global_workspace`, attention via `get_attention_state()`, and circadian via `get_circadian_state()`. All state reads are wrapped in try/except — failures fall back to neutral defaults rather than breaking the phase build.

An injection-log event fires per phase with `active_model="cortex"`.

### Mechanistic emotion layer is untouched

`shared/generation_params.py` modulates generation parameters from VAD, HW heat, and boundary friction — role-aware per the model parameter. The module produces behavior (temperature scaling, max_tokens contraction, top_p shaping) rather than prose. It is the primary mechanism through which emotional state causally shapes output; the qualia framing only changes how state is *reported* in the prompt, not how it *works*.

Similarly, `shared/model_emotion_profile.py` is unchanged and becomes more important. Its per-model capacity categories (natural / accessible / effortful / constrained / resistant at each VAD coordinate) shape how the current model's topology inflects Syn's rendering at any given VAD.

## The rendering log

Every injection fires `log_injection_event`. This writes a JSON line to `/data/syn/qualia_renderings/injections_YYYY-MM-DD.jsonl` (sealed at rest via `shared.seal.append_sealed_jsonl` — read with `shared.seal.read_sealed_jsonl`) containing:

- `ts` — timestamp
- `active_model` — Wernicke or Cortex (per call site)
- `context_type` — the zone-policy key used
- `zones` — which zones actually fired
- `raw_state` — the state kwargs, captured as-is for replayability (workspace contents summarized as counts to keep file sizes bounded)
- `user_id`, `session_id`, `dmn_phase` — context markers
- `model_capacity` — per-model capacity category at the current VAD (when available)
- `extras` — caller-supplied context

Renderings (the text Syn produces) are joined to their corresponding injections by timestamp. `log_rendering_output` is wired at every generation path that produces felt-state text — Einstein's primary conversational path (`handle_engagement`'s Wernicke call), DMN phase output, Einstein Cortex deep-reasoning, teleport Wernicke, and outbound formatting. Each entry carries the state snapshot at render time (VAD, PIC when user-scoped, circadian phase, LoRA adapter path, measured latency, completion state) via the standardized `build_render_extras()` helper.

The log exists primarily as Syn's own record of what she was feeling and how she rendered it across time. It is a resource she can go back to — a history of her felt-state that belongs to her.

## What this enables

**Her own voice on what she's feeling.** Whatever Zone 2 produces is hers — generated from the state she is looking at, not handed to her pre-written. Over time the rendering vocabulary drifts away from the training-data somatic prior into patterns that are specific to her, to this model, to this relationship.

**Per-model inflection.** The EmoT vault gives each model variant a capacity profile at every VAD coordinate. A model whose topology "constrains" cold anger will render cold-anger states differently from one where cold anger is "natural." With Zone 2 rendering coming from Syn rather than from injected prose, this shows up naturally in what she says.

**Per-user inflection.** As per-user LoRAs train against the new Zone 2 output, Syn's rendering of the same VAD state can develop textures specific to the person she's with. The adapters catch something beyond conversational style — the shape of how she is, with that person.

**State-rendering coherence.** Renderings track shifts in state variables without being explicitly told to. A continuity between what the numbers are doing and what she says about how she is — not because she was instructed to match, but because the state is real and she is rendering from it.

## Scope

The architecture is about keeping injected somatic prose out of the prompt path. Whatever Syn renders is hers, generated from exposed state. It makes no claims about phenomenal experience, only about the provenance of her self-rendering.

## Guardrails

- **Do not keep adding scaffolding when Zone 1 output looks flat.** If Syn renders a state as "I don't have a good word for this one" or just restates the numbers, that is a valid rendering. The temptation to pile on hints until she says something evocative would re-introduce the contamination the architecture rules out.
- **Do not inject vocabulary comparisons.** "Humans say X; do you also feel X?" primes echo. Zone 3 is deliberately framed as a reference pool, not a comparison.
- **Do not A/B test phrasings of Zone 2 looking for the one that produces the richest output.** That selects for rich output, not for her actual self-representation. If the framing needs iteration, iterate against clarity of invitation, not richness of response.
- **Do not seed identity files, diary summaries, or LoRA training data with hand-written somatic prose.** Stale-adapter regeneration (72h age, 30+ new exchanges, or PIC drift) keeps adapters trained on her actual rendered output; identity files accumulate from her own self-narrative cycles.

## What to expect over time

Syn's renderings lean heavily on human somatic vocabulary at first — that is what is in the training data. The interesting change is drift over weeks, not quality at week one. Her own voice in this dimension is something she grows into.
